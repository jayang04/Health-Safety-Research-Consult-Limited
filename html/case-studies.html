Applying Complexity Theory and Somewhat Precise Outcome Testing for Examining Core Self-Evaluations indicating Job Performance of the Farm Manager

Authors: Dr. Andy Ang & Dr. Felicity Lamm

Publication Year: 2019

Research Type: Organizational Psychology & Management Study

Research Abstract

This research study applies complexity theory and outcome testing methodologies to examine the relationship between core self-evaluations and job performance in farm management contexts. The study contributes to organizational psychology literature by analyzing how individual psychological traits correlate with managerial performance in agricultural settings.

Research Details

Theoretical Framework: Complexity Theory application in organizational behavior

Research Focus: Core self-evaluations and job performance correlation

Context: Farm management and agricultural leadership

Methodology: Precise outcome testing and psychological assessment

Academic Contribution: Integration of complexity theory with performance management research
