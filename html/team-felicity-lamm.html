Dr. Felicity Lamm

Senior Research Partner

About

Associate Professor Dr. Felicity Lamm was the inaugural Co-Director of the Centre for Occupational Health and Safety Research at the Auckland University of Technology (AUT) between 2007 and 2024. In 2018 she was also appointed the Chief Advisor Occupational Health and Safety for the New Zealand Government. In 2025 she was awarded both the AIRAANZ Vic Taylor Lifetime Achievement Award and Safeguard Lifetime Achievement Award.

She has been teaching and researching in the areas of occupational health and safety (OHS), risk management and employment relations for over 30 years. As Co-Director of the Centre, she has led numerous collaborative research and consultancy projects and has established support networks for researchers and OHS practitioners and professionals.

Research & Expertise

Dr. Lamm has written extensively on occupational health and safety, compiling research reports for New Zealand and overseas public and private sector organisations. Her research covers:

  • Regulating and complying with employment and OHS law in small business sector
  • OHS issues in agricultural, construction, forestry, mining, fishing and service sectors
  • Risk management and employment relations
  • Worker representation and participation
  • Road safety strategy for vehicles as workplace environments

Government Advisory Roles

  • Ministerial Inquiry into Tranz Rail Occupational Health and Safety
  • Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment Worker Representation and Participation Guidance Group
  • Expert Adviser - Ministry of Transport's Road Safety Strategy: Vehicles as a Workplace
