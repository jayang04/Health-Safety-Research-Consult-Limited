About Us

Dedicated to promoting health, safety and wellness among working individuals through professional research and consultancy capacity in occupational health and safety across New Zealand, Asia, and the Pacific.

Our Mission

Health & Safety Research and Consultancy is dedicated to health and safety in the workforce. Our mission is to promote health, safety and wellness among working individuals. Priority has been given to building professional research and consultancy capacity in OHS, not only in New Zealand but also in Asia and the Pacific.

We address the challenges facing the 21st-century workforce, providing new knowledge and evidence-based solutions that improve worker health, well-being, and productivity, in the region, and beyond. We harness the tools of basic, clinical, and applied research to promote wellness and prevent disease and disability among workers across industries.

What We Do

We conduct safety climate, well-being, and psychosocial risks research at your workplace. We investigate managing health and safety within culturally diverse workplaces. We focus on key areas critical to worker health and well-being: environmental exposure, supportive workplace practices, and workplace safety.

Collaboration with you is essential to how we provide evidence-based solutions to improve the health and well-being of workers and the productivity of your organisation. We partner with employees and employers, government agencies, and industry groups to understand and address the health and safety-related challenges facing the 21st-century workforce.

Our Key Focus Areas:

  • Safety Climate Research: Serving as a key indicator of safety outcomes, highlighting the relationship between safety decisions and production objectives
  • People at Work Tool: A 5-step process to help leaders identify, assess, and manage risks to psychological health in the workplace
  • Psychosocial Risk Assessment: Detailed assessment of workplace psychosocial hazards such as bullying, harassment, and workload stressors
  • Prevention Programs: Targeting areas with disproportionate injuries or fatalities, developing people-centered interventions

Meet Our Team

Dr. Andy (Huat Bin) Ang (PhD)

Lead Researcher & Consultant

Dr. Andy Ang is a researcher and consultant specialising in workplace health and safety, as well as the well-being of workers. He possesses the expertise to conduct on-site safety research in occupational health and safety (OHS). Dr. Ang has authored reports on a variety of topics, including the Pike River Mine tragedy, challenges faced by temporary and vulnerable workers, mental health initiatives, and issues related to zoos, museums, the health sector, OHS leadership in the manufacturing sector, and safety within the primary sector, including kiwifruit and avocado packhouses.

Over the past 15 years, he has collaborated with various organisations in New Zealand and overseas, including CHASNZ, HASNZ, ETU, ACC, WorkSafe, City Council, Federated Farmers, Horticulture New Zealand, the Mental Health Foundation, and Victoria University of Wellington. Additionally, Dr. Andy Ang is a team member of the Safety Climate Lab at Oregon Health and Science University. He participated in a significant project with an overseas university and the Safety Climate Lab, focusing on mobile-health in manufacturing. This includes involvement in the Total Health Worker Project on Safety Climate and Safety Culture.

Alice Ang

Research Assistant

She is an experienced Research Assistant with a proven record of supporting projects that prioritise safety, accuracy, and operational excellence. Her work includes conducting research for organisations such as Hamilton Zoo and local museums, managing data collection, designing and analysing online surveys, and producing comprehensive reports using Microsoft Word and Excel. She collaborates effectively with multidisciplinary teams to ensure all information is accurate, well-documented, and aligned with organisational standards.
