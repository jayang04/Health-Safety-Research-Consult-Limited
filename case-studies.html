Applying Complexity Theory and Somewhat Precise Outcome Testing for Examining Core Self-Evaluations indicating Job Performance of the Farm Manager

Authors: Dr. Andy Ang & Dr. Felicity Lamm

Publication Year: 2019

Research Type: Organizational Psychology & Management Study

Research Abstract

This research study applies complexity theory and outcome testing methodologies to examine the relationship between core self-evaluations and job performance in farm management contexts. The study contributes to organizational psychology literature by analyzing how individual psychological traits correlate with managerial performance in agricultural settings.

Research Details

Theoretical Framework: Complexity Theory application in organizational behavior

Research Focus: Core self-evaluations and job performance correlation

Context: Farm management and agricultural leadership

Methodology: Precise outcome testing and psychological assessment

Academic Contribution: Integration of complexity theory with performance management research

Pain Pal: Mindfulness-Based Chronic Pain Management Application

Developer: Dr. Thomas S. Nasser & ZenZone Interactive

Collaboration Focus: Digital Health & Pain Management Innovation

Research Type: Mindfulness-Based Pain Management Technology

Project Overview

Pain Pal represents a paradigm shift in pain management, offering an innovative app-based solution that provides an effective alternative to opioids in managing chronic pain symptoms. This collaboration between Dr. Thomas S. Nasser and ZenZone Interactive delivers mindfulness-based pain management through cutting-edge interactive technology.

Key Features & Methodology

Core Technology: Interactive guided mindfulness with biometric integration

Treatment Approach: Combination of attention, breathing, and acceptance techniques

Specialized Sessions: Increase Mobility, Better Sleep with Pain, Healthy Eating for Pain Management

Clinical Foundation: 15+ years of pain diagnosis and treatment experience

Academic Support: Developed with guidance from UCL University neuroscientists and psychologists
